CHEMICALS

  • For Chemical Burns, cool water should be run over the area until the entire chemical is removed.

  • Splashed Chemical on the Skin. Immediately rinse the area with cold water for at least one minute. Consult your instructor for further action.

EYE RESCUE

  • Accidental Acid Splash on the Eye. Immediately wash the affected eye with clean fresh water (close unaffected eye to protect it also.) Open your affected eye and allow the water to freely rinse the acid, use the tub when available. Avoid rubbing the eyes from irritation. If irritation continues, consult an eye specialist.

  • Particle in your Eye. Apply above instruction or you can use a hair to remove particle. Provide long hair from your mates then fold and hold both end. Locate the particle in the eye (let the victim look up-sidedown until the particle becomes visible) and let hair lift the particle gently. If irritation continues, consult an eye specialist.

CUTS

  • Rinse with Water thoroughly then apply 50% denatured alcohol or tincture of iodine. Provide bandage with sterile gauze and clean cotton cloth. Avoid continued used of iodine in subsequent dressings otherwise burns will result. Never cover directly with adhesive tape.

BURNS

  • First Aid for burn requires cooling by plain water, ice may include shock. After this, some ointment may help soothe a minor burn, but ointments or greasy substances should never be applied to serious burns.

  • For Chemical burns, cool water should be run over the area until the entire chemical is removed. Cover with solid sodium bicarbonate. Apply dry dressing then consult your physician.

FIRE

  • For small fires, locate the gas valve and turn it off. Then apply a wet towel to smother the fire. If this fails, use the fire extinguisher provided for this purpose.

  • Flames on clothing. Smother fire by covering yourself with a jacket or by rolling on the floor. Don't panic, report fire immediately for rescue.

POISON

  • Inhaled poison. When there's no gas mask available, take a long breath and hold before moving the victim to the open area and seek help. Loosen the victim's clothing then apply CPR until rescue arrives.

BITES

  • Animal, insect, and human bites and stings easily become infected and must be carefully cleaned and washed. The possibility of tetanus and rabies must be considered, and a physician should be quickly consulted.
